## Quick diagnosis

Use this triage:

1. Compare **usage units** (kWh, therms, or gallons), not just dollars.
2. Compare same month last year (seasonality matters).
3. Check if your **rate changed** or if there are new fees.
4. Look for one new high-load habit/appliance.

If usage is flat but cost rose, the issue is often rate/fees. If usage rose sharply, the issue is usually behavior, equipment, or leaks.

---

## Top causes

- Seasonal demand changes (HVAC, water heating)
- Rate or tariff changes
- Equipment inefficiency
- Hidden continuous usage (leak, standby loads, stuck settings)
- Billing cycle differences or estimate-to-actual corrections

## Step-by-step fix order

### 1) Validate the bill
- Confirm billing dates and meter read type (actual vs estimated).
- Check provider notices for rate updates.

### 2) Identify the biggest load window
- Review hourly/daily usage graph if your utility offers one.
- Pinpoint spikes and map them to activities.

### 3) Run one focused test
- For 48 hours, reduce one major load (HVAC setpoint, water heater temp, laundry timing).
- Track usage response.

### 4) Check hardware health
- Filter condition, thermostat calibration, water heater settings, appliance age.

### 5) Lock in long-term controls
- Schedule-based usage, efficient replacements, better rate plan.

## Cost and savings math

A simple formula:

`Savings = (old monthly usage - new monthly usage) × effective rate`

Example:
- Usage drop: 220 kWh
- Effective rate: $0.18/kWh
- Monthly savings: **$39.60**
- Annualized: **$475.20**

Track changes for at least two billing cycles before concluding.

## When to call utility support

Call your provider when:
- You suspect meter or billing mismatch
- Usage is impossible given occupancy
- You need tariff/rate reassignment help
- You want an energy audit or leak review program

## FAQ

### What matters more: usage or price?
Usage first. Then rate. A small rate increase can look huge if usage also climbed.

### How fast can I see bill improvements?
Behavior changes may show in days, but billing proof usually takes 1–2 cycles.

### Should I switch plans immediately?
Only after checking contract terms, peak windows, and historical usage profile.

### Is one high bill always a problem?
Not always. Billing-cycle length and weather outliers can produce one-off spikes.
